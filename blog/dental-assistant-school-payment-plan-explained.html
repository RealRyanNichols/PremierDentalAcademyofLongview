The two options, side by side

Option one: pay in full. $3,000, once, done. This is the lowest total cost — you save $500 compared to the plan.

Option two: the payment plan. $3,500 total. You put $500 down to hold your seat, then pay the $3,000 balance in simple weekly or monthly payments (up to about 12 months). Your certificate is issued once tuition is paid in full. That's the entire structure — no interest rate mysteries, no balloon payment at the end.

Why the plan costs $500 more

Carrying payments over months has real administrative cost, and we'd rather price it honestly up front than hide it in fees. If you can pay in full, do — it's the better deal. If you can't, the plan exists so a lump sum never has to be the thing standing between you and a career.

What the $500 down actually does

It holds your seat. Classes are capped at eight students, so a seat held is a seat someone else can't take — the deposit is what makes your spot real on our roster, triggers your welcome materials, and starts your enrollment paperwork. It also counts toward your $3,500 total; it's not a fee on top.

If neither option fits your budget yet

Don't quietly walk away — there are real funding routes. Texas Workforce Solutions vouchers and WIOA funding cover training costs for eligible students, and we accept GI Bill benefits. Start with the step-by-step voucher guide or the WIOA overview. And checkout offers split-pay options (Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm) if those suit you better. What we'll never do: invent a discount to pressure you. The price is the price, for everyone.

The online option

The self-paced online program is $397 right now (regularly $997), starts any day, and the full $397 transfers as credit toward in-person tuition within 90 days if you decide you want the hands-on experience. A low-risk way to start today.
