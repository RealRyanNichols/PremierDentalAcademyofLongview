Start with the real numbers

In-person tuition is $3,000 paid in full or $3,500 on the payment plan — $500 down, then the $3,000 balance split into weekly or monthly payments you choose (full mechanics in how the payment plan works). The self-paced online program is $397 right now (regularly $997). Those are the only numbers — no application fees, no textbook surprises sprung at orientation.

The funding routes people forget exist

Workforce Solutions vouchers and WIOA funding pay training costs for eligible East Texans — many people qualify who assume they don't, especially career-changers and anyone recently laid off. The process takes some paperwork and lead time, so start early: the voucher walkthrough and the WIOA guide show exactly how. Veterans: we accept GI Bill benefits. And checkout supports split-pay options (Klarna, Afterpay, Affirm) if spreading it that way suits you better.

What we won't do

No fake "scholarship deadlines," no countdown-timer discounts, no pressure calls. The price is the price for everyone, and it'll be the same price after you've had a week to think. Budget decisions made under pressure are usually bad ones — take the time you need.

Budget the life around school, too

Tuition is the visible cost; the invisible ones are gas for two or three class days a week, maybe some childcare hours, and scrubs. Most students find these smaller than feared — daytime classes just two or three days weekly leave room to keep working through the program, which is how the majority of our students pay their plan. If building any cushion at all feels impossible right now, start with the first-$500 savings starter — it was written for exactly that feeling.

The return side of the math

A budget has two columns, and the second one matters: twelve weeks after starting, you're a trained dental assistant in a field that hires steadily across East Texas. What that's worth in pay depends on the office and your growth — estimate your own range honestly with the salary guide and weigh it in is dental assistant school worth it.
