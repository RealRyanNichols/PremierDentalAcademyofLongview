September or November? How to pick your cohort start date

Four in-person start dates remain in 2026: September 14 (MWF), September 29 (T/Th), November 9 (MWF), and November 17 (T/Th). Here's how to actually choose — because "later" has a way of becoming "never," and both months have a real case.

First: the two questions that matter more than the month

Question one: which days can you actually protect? Each month offers both rhythms — Monday/Wednesday/Friday or Tuesday/Thursday. If your work schedule, childcare, or other commitments make two days easier to guard than three, that answers half the decision by itself. Question two: when do you want to be job-hunting? The program runs about twelve weeks, so count forward: a September start has you interviewing around the end of the year; a November start puts you in the market in early spring. Both work — they're just different finish lines.

The honest case for not waiting

Here's the pattern we see every single enrollment cycle: the people who pick the later date "to get ready" are usually no more ready in November than they were in September — because readiness mostly comes from starting, not before it. There's no prerequisite homework, no fitness test, nothing to prepare that the first two weeks don't teach you. If both months genuinely work for your life, take the earlier date and be twelve weeks ahead of the version of you that waited.

The real deadline is seat count, not the calendar

Every cohort is capped at eight students — that's a teaching decision, not scarcity theater. But it does mean a cohort can fill before its start date. If a specific date matters to you, the $500 seat hold (or paying in full) is what makes it yours.

Holidays, kids, and real life

A November start does cross the holidays, and we build the schedule knowing that — students aren't asked to choose between class and Thanksgiving dinner. Some students actually prefer training through the holiday season: the structure keeps momentum through a time of year when job searches slow down anyway, and you come out the other side credentialed exactly when offices are setting their new-year budgets and filling positions. Parents juggling school pickups: daytime classes two or three days a week are more compatible with a kid schedule than most jobs are — here's how other parents made it work.
