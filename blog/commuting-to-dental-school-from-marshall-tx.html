Commuting to dental assistant school from Marshall, TX

Marshall doesn't have its own dental assistant school — and that stops a lot of good people before they start. Here's the honest math on training in Longview when you live in Marshall: what the drive involves, how to make it count, and why it's a 12-week commitment, not a lifestyle.

The drive, honestly

Marshall to our Longview campus (2800 Gilmer Rd, Suite 106) is a straight shot most Marshall folks already know — for many it's roughly a half-hour behind the wheel each way, depending on where in Marshall you start and which route you take. It's real time, and we won't pretend otherwise. But two things put it in perspective: classes meet only two or three days a week (MWF or T/Th cohorts), and the whole program is about twelve weeks. This is a season of driving, not a new way of life.

Make the windshield time work for you

The commute study hall

Terminology runs beautifully on a drive. Quiz yourself out loud on the day's terms, replay procedure sequences in your head, or have a family member run flashcards with you the night before and recite on the road. Students who treat the drive as review time tell us it stopped feeling like lost time entirely.

Practical points that help: T/Th cohorts mean only two driving days a week if your schedule is tight; carpooling with another Marshall-area student cuts costs (ask us — we often know who's coming from where); and daytime classes mean you're not driving home exhausted at midnight. Call or text (903) 913-6444 for exact class hours before you plan.

The part people miss: the jobs question

Here's the quiet advantage. Train in Longview and you graduate with connections in both markets — Longview's larger pool of offices and Marshall's own practices, which often have a harder time finding trained help. Some graduates work in Longview for the wider options; others take everything they learned back to a Marshall office five minutes from home. Either way the commute ends at graduation, and the credential doesn't. More on how geography shapes options: the commute and jobs guide.

Is it worth it?

Twelve weeks of half-hour drives versus a career you can build on for decades, in a field that hires steadily across East Texas — you can do that math yourself. If the drive is truly impossible, the self-paced online program exists precisely for that. But most Marshall students who tour the campus once stop worrying about the drive.
