Longview to Tyler: is commuting for a dental job worth it?

Tyler has more dental offices; Longview has your house. East Texas assistants weigh this trade every year. Here is an honest way to run the numbers — including a calculator that does the math with YOUR figures, not ours.

The real cost of a commute isn't just gas

A Longview–Tyler drive is roughly 40 minutes each way along I-20 or Highway 31 depending on where you start and end. That is about an hour and twenty minutes of your day — call it 6–7 hours a week of unpaid time — plus fuel, plus wear on your car, plus childcare minutes if someone else is watching your kids while you drive. A slightly higher hourly rate in Tyler can absolutely be worth it. It can also quietly evaporate on the highway.

When the drive makes sense

Commute for the RIGHT reasons.

A specialty office that teaches you expanded skills, a mentor doctor, meaningfully better benefits, or a clear path to a raise — those compound over a career and can justify the highway. A flat dollar-an-hour bump usually does not survive the calculator above.

Also remember the market is not one-sided: offices in Longview, Marshall, Kilgore, Henderson, and Gladewater hire assistants year-round, and our graduates work all over Gregg and Smith counties.
