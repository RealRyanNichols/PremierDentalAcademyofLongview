A walkthrough of the free tuition planner tool

The scariest part of going back to school is usually the money — not because of the number itself, but because of the not-knowing. The tuition planner kills the not-knowing: it shows your exact payment schedule, date by date, using the same math checkout uses. Before you've committed to anything.

The numbers it plans around

The planner works with the real, current tuition — nothing hypothetical. In-person training is $3,000 paid in full, or $3,500 on a plan: $500 down to hold your seat, then the $3,000 balance in weekly or monthly payments. Those are the only numbers that exist, and the planner never shows you anything checkout wouldn't honor.

Same math as checkout — on purpose

A lot of schools show you a friendly estimate online and a different number at signing. The planner runs the identical calculation our enrollment checkout runs, so the schedule you build is the schedule you'd actually pay. No bait, no switch.

Why seeing the schedule changes the decision

"Thirty-five hundred dollars" is abstract and heavy. "A payment I chose, on dates I chose, that ends on a date I can see" is a plan — and plans feel possible. Students tell us the planner is the moment school stopped being a someday-wish and became a calendar entry. That's the point of the tool: replace dread with information, and let you make the decision with real numbers in front of you.

It's also a family conversation tool. If a spouse or parent is part of the decision, printing or screenshotting your schedule turns "can we afford this?" into a concrete talk about actual dates and amounts instead of guesses.

If the schedule still doesn't fit

That's information too — and there are more doors. Texas Workforce Solutions and WIOA funding cover training for eligible students (start with the WIOA guide), GI Bill benefits are accepted, and the $500-cushion savings plan shows a realistic way to build the down payment.
