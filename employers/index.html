What our graduates train to do

Across the program, students build and practice these chairside and front-office competencies:

✓Infection control & sterilization — PPE, surface barriers, instrument processing and spore testing
✓Operatory setup & turnover — stocking, waterline flushing, room readiness
✓Seating patients & vitals — comfort, medical history, blood pressure and pulse
✓Charting — existing restorations, periodontal probing, Universal tooth numbering
✓Radiography — sensor positioning, bitewing series, recognizing and correcting x-ray errors
✓Tray setup — basic exam, restorative and procedure-specific trays
✓Four-handed assisting — instrument transfer, isolation, retraction and evacuation
✓Procedure assisting — crown prep, composite restorations and simple extractions
✓Impressions & materials — alginate impressions, model pouring, cements and bonding
✓Patient communication — professional phone skills and post-operative instructions
✓Office software — opening charts, scheduling and front-desk workflows
✓Emergency readiness — locating the emergency kit, O₂/AED and responding to syncope

Individual graduates vary in which competencies they've completed and which an instructor has verified. We'll share each candidate's Competency Passport so there are no surprises.
