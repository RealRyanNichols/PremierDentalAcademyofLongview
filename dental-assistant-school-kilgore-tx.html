What a dental assistant actually does

A dental assistant is the dentist's right hand and the patient's calming presence. It's a hands-on, people-facing job with a real rhythm to the day. A typical shift looks something like this:

  • • Setting up treatment rooms and sterilizing instruments between patients
  • • Seating patients, taking health histories, and helping them feel at ease
  • • Handing instruments and assisting chairside during fillings, crowns, and cleanings
  • • Taking dental X-rays and charting what the dentist finds in the office software
  • • Explaining home-care instructions and helping schedule the next visit at the front desk

No two days are exactly alike, and you're on your feet helping people the whole time. It's steady, indoor, daytime work that's in demand in just about every town with a dental office — Kilgore included.

What you'll learn

The whole point is to walk into an interview already able to do the job. You'll build the core skills offices look for:

  • ✓ Charting — reading and recording tooth-by-tooth findings the way the dentist expects
  • ✓ Radiology basics — the fundamentals of taking and handling dental X-rays safely
  • ✓ Chairside assisting — instruments, trays, suction, and four-handed dentistry
  • ✓ Front-office software — scheduling, patient records, and practice-management tools
  • ✓ Infection control — sterilization, PPE, and keeping every room safe
