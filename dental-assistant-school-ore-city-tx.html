What you'll learn

In-person or online, the curriculum is the same set of skills offices hire for:

  • ✓ Charting — reading the tooth chart and recording conditions and treatment accurately.
  • ✓ Radiology basics — how dental x-rays work and how they fit into patient care.
  • ✓ Chairside assisting — setting up, passing instruments, and supporting the dentist during procedures.
  • ✓ Front-office software — the practice-management tools offices use to schedule, chart, and bill.
  • ✓ Infection control — sterilization and safety the way real offices run it.

Want to test-drive it before you spend a dollar? The free Texas RDA practice exam and our charting trainer show you exactly what the work feels like.

Ore City-area FAQs

How long is the drive from Ore City?

About 22 miles down US-259 — roughly 30 minutes to our campus at 2800 Gilmer Rd, Suite 106, Longview. You come in on the northwest side of town, which is where the campus sits.

Is there a class schedule that works with a job?

Yes — flexible class options are designed for working adults. Train after work and still get home to Upshur County at a decent hour. The online program ($397, self-paced) is the fallback if the schedule doesn't fit.

What do dental assistants around here earn?

Pay varies by office and experience — see our salary page for typical East Texas numbers rather than a promise. Offices in Longview, Gilmer, and Jefferson all hire within commuting range of Ore City.

Do I need anything before I enroll?

No degree and no dental experience. Bring the willingness to learn; we cover charting, radiology basics, chairside assisting, infection control, and front-office software from scratch.
