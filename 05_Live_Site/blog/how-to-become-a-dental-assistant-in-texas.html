How to become a dental assistant in Texas (2026)

A start-to-finish roadmap — from your first day of training to your first day on the job in an East Texas dental office.

Dental assisting is one of the fastest ways into a stable, well-paying healthcare career in Texas. You don't need a four-year degree, you can train in a matter of weeks instead of years, and demand across East Texas — Longview, Tyler, Marshall, Kilgore — stays consistently strong. This guide walks you through exactly what it takes to become a dental assistant in Texas in 2026, step by step.

Dental assistant vs. Registered Dental Assistant (RDA)

First, an important distinction. In Texas, "dental assistant" and "Registered Dental Assistant" are not the same thing.

  • A dental assistant can perform basic, non-restricted chairside and front-desk duties without state registration.
  • A Registered Dental Assistant (RDA) has completed approved training and registered with the Texas State Board of Dental Examiners (TSBDE). Only an RDA can legally take dental X-rays, monitor nitrous oxide, coronal polish, and assist in certain expanded procedures.

Here's the reality on the ground: nearly every dental office in East Texas prefers to hire RDAs, because a registered assistant can do far more the moment they walk in the door. If you want to be competitive — and paid well — becoming an RDA is the goal. For a deeper look at that specific process, see our Texas RDA registration guide.

The requirements at a glance

To register as an RDA in Texas in 2026, you'll need to satisfy each of the following through the TSBDE:

  • A high school diploma or GED.
  • A TSBDE-approved dental assistant training course covering radiography (X-ray positioning and exposure), infection control, and jurisprudence.
  • A hands-on Basic Life Support (BLS) course. Note: Texas does not accept online-only BLS — it must include an in-person, hands-on component.
  • A human trafficking prevention course. Required for all TSBDE applicants since October 1, 2021.
  • A criminal history background check via submitted fingerprints.
  • Your application and fees submitted through the TSBDE online system.

There's also an alternate path: if you already hold a current DANB Certified Dental Assistant (CDA) credential, you can register by passing the Texas Jurisprudence Assessment within one year of applying.

Step-by-step: your path to RDA

  1. Confirm you meet the basics. Have your high school diploma or GED ready. That's the only academic prerequisite.
  2. Enroll in an approved training program. This is where you learn the clinical skills and complete the radiology, infection control, and jurisprudence components Texas requires. A quality program also gets you real chairside practice — not just theory.
  3. Earn your radiology certification. The TSBDE-approved radiography course is a focused unit (commonly around six hours of instruction plus an exam, with a set window to complete it). You must pass to legally take X-rays.
  4. Complete a hands-on BLS/CPR course. Keep the card current — most offices require it as a condition of employment.
  5. Finish the human trafficking prevention course. A short, board-approved course — free options that meet the 2026 requirement exist.
  6. Submit fingerprints for your background check. Follow the TSBDE's designated fingerprinting vendor instructions.
  7. Apply to TSBDE and pay your fees. Submit your completed application, proof of training, and fees through the board's online portal.
  8. Receive your registration and start working. Once approved, you can perform the full scope of RDA duties.

Frequently asked questions

Can I work in a dental office before I'm registered?

Yes — Texas allows dental assistants to perform basic, non-restricted duties while they train. You simply can't take X-rays or perform RDA-restricted tasks until your registration is issued.

Do I need a college degree?

No. A high school diploma or GED plus an approved training course is all Texas requires.

Does my RDA registration expire?

Yes. RDAs renew every two years and must complete at least 12 hours of continuing education (6 of them clinical), including a board-approved human trafficking prevention course as part of that total.

What if I'm moving to Texas with experience from another state?

You'll still need to meet Texas's specific requirements. Holding a current DANB CDA credential can streamline the process — you can register by passing the Texas Jurisprudence Assessment within a year of applying.
