How much do dental assistants actually make in East Texas? (2026 data)

Real numbers from BLS + a survey of 12 Longview-area offices. No estimates, no fluff.

Short answer: A brand-new Registered Dental Assistant in East Texas starts around $17–$22 per hour, or roughly $36,000–$46,000 per year. With 3+ years of experience and the right skills, that climbs to $48,000–$58,000. Lead RDAs and office managers regularly hit $65,000+.

The longer answer matters more — because what determines where you land in that range is mostly under your control.

The base numbers (BLS + local survey)

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics reports the 2024 median dental-assistant wage in Texas at $19.83/hr ($41,250/yr). We surveyed 12 East Texas offices in March 2026 and got numbers slightly higher than the BLS median — because our region pays a small premium for skilled hires given the hiring shortage.

5 things that bump your pay faster than waiting

1. Practice management software fluency

Every Longview-area office we surveyed said the same thing: the new hire who already knows Dentrix or EagleSoft saves us six weeks of training, so we pay them more from day one. Most schools don't teach this. PDA does (that's literally why we exist).

2. X-ray certification

In Texas, you need the Dental Assistant Radiology certification to take X-rays. Without it, you're stuck doing front-desk work or chairside-assisting only. With it, you're a full RDA — and pay reflects that. PDA includes radiology in both programs.

3. CPR / BLS

Required at most offices. Free or low-cost certifications at the Red Cross. We'll point you to the closest provider during the program.

4. Speaking Spanish

East Texas dental offices serve a large Spanish-speaking patient population. Bilingual RDAs routinely earn $2–$5/hr more in our market. If you already speak Spanish, lead with it in your resume.

5. Insurance / billing skills

The single biggest production-killer at any practice is messy insurance work. RDAs who can submit claims, post EOBs, and follow up on denials are gold. This is a full module in our curriculum — every PDA student practices it on real ADA claim forms.

What about benefits?

Most full-time RDA positions in East Texas include:

  • Health insurance — about 70% of offices contribute toward a plan
  • Paid time off — typically 5–10 days starting, growing with tenure
  • Free / discounted dental work for you and immediate family — this alone can save you $2,000–$5,000 per year
  • Uniform allowance at many offices
  • Retirement match at larger DSO-owned practices

Add those benefits to a $42,000 base salary and the real total compensation is often $50,000+.

Where the highest-paying jobs are in our region

Based on March 2026 survey responses:

  • Longview: highest median rate in East Texas (urban core, more specialists)
  • Tyler: close second; large multi-doctor practices pay top of range
  • Marshall, Kilgore, Henderson: slightly lower median but lower cost of living balances out
  • Specialty practices (oral surgery, ortho, perio, pedo) typically pay 10–20% more than general dentistry
