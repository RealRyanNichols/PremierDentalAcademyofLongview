🦷 Instrument cheat sheet

Mouth mirrorIndirect vision, retract the cheek/tongue, reflect light.
ExplorerDetect decay, calculus, and check margins by feel.
Cotton pliersPick up and place small items (cotton, wedges) in the mouth.
Periodontal probeMeasure sulcus/pocket depth in millimeters.
Spoon excavatorRemove soft decay and temporary cement.
Condenser (plugger)Pack and condense restorative material into the prep.
Carver (e.g. discoid-cleoid)Shape tooth anatomy into the restoration before it sets.
BurnisherSmooth and adapt the margins of a restoration.
Amalgam carrierCarry and dispense amalgam to the prep.
HVE tipHigh-volume evacuation — keeps the field dry, removes debris/aerosol.
Saliva ejectorLow-volume suction for patient comfort and pooled saliva.

🧰 Tray setups

Basic exam

Mouth mirror · explorer · cotton pliers · (gauze, cotton rolls).

Composite / restorative

Exam set · spoon excavator · condenser · carver · burnisher · composite instrument · matrix & wedge · bite block.

Crown & bridge

Exam set · spoon excavator · condenser · carver · impression/scan supplies · temporary material · bite block.

Extraction

Mouth mirror · anesthetic syringe · elevators · forceps · gauze · (surgical curette as needed).

Endodontics (root canal)

Exam set (mirror · explorer · cotton pliers) · anesthetic syringe · files · rubber dam setup · bite block.

Always remember

Set up in order of use, keep the field dry, and follow your operatory's protocol. Practice it in the Tray Builder.

🔢 Tooth numbering (Universal System)

Permanent teeth: numbered 1–32. #1 = upper-right third molar (wisdom), counting across the top to #16 (upper-left), then dropping down to #17 (lower-left third molar) and across to #32 (lower-right).

Quadrants: Upper right 1–8 · Upper left 9–16 · Lower left 17–24 · Lower right 25–32.

Primary (baby) teeth: lettered A–T, same path — A = upper-right second molar to J (upper-left), K (lower-left) to T (lower-right).

Tip: #8 and #9 are your two upper front central incisors — a fast way to orient yourself on any chart.

📋 State-board quick study

Infection control

Hand hygiene, PPE (gloves, mask, eyewear), surface barriers, and instrument processing: clean → package → sterilize (autoclave) → store. Dirty vs. clean flow is one-direction. Spore tests verify the autoclave.

Radiology safety

ALARA — As Low As Reasonably Achievable. Lead apron + thyroid collar, correct exposure, and never hold the sensor for the patient. Know film/PSP/sensor handling.

Chairside

Four-handed dentistry, instrument transfer, isolation and moisture control, and anticipating the dentist's next instrument. Tray set up in order of use.

This is a study aid. Your state board's official requirements always govern — confirm specifics with your instructor and the Texas State Board of Dental Examiners.
