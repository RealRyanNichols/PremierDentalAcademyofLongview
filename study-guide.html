How to use this guide

Work the topics below in order. For each one, read the focus note, then prove it with our free practice exam and flashcards. When a topic still feels shaky, that's your signal to drill it more — the exam rewards consistency, not cramming.

The topics to master

  1. Infection control & safety — standard precautions, PPE, sterilization vs. disinfection, instrument processing, OSHA basics. High-yield: know it cold.
  2. Dental radiography (X-rays) — exposure technique, paralleling vs. bisecting, errors and retakes, ALARA, patient protection.
  3. Dental anatomy & tooth numbering — Universal numbering (1–32), surfaces, primary vs. permanent, landmarks.
  4. Chairside assisting — four-handed technique, instrument transfer, isolation, moisture control, tray setups by procedure.
  5. Dental materials — amalgam, composite, cements, impression materials — mixing, handling, and when each is used.
  6. Charting & dental terminology — abbreviations, conditions vs. treatment, existing vs. needed work. Practice with our charting trainer.
  7. Patient management — medical histories, vitals, comfort, communication, special-needs considerations.
  8. Office procedures & records — appointment flow, recordkeeping, confidentiality (HIPAA basics).
  9. Texas jurisprudence & scope of practice — what an RDA may and may not do in Texas; know the rules.
  10. Medical emergencies — recognizing and responding to common in-office emergencies; the emergency kit.

A simple 2-week plan

  • Days 1–3: Infection control + radiography (the heaviest-weighted areas).
  • Days 4–7: Anatomy, charting, and materials — flashcards daily.
  • Days 8–10: Chairside, patient management, office/records.
  • Days 11–12: Jurisprudence + emergencies.
  • Days 13–14: Full timed practice exams; re-drill anything under 75%.
